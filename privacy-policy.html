3.2 Consent Before SDK Initialization (EEA/UK/Switzerland)

In jurisdictions where prior consent is required before accessing or storing information on your device (including under the ePrivacy Directive, Article 5(3)), we do not initialize the advertising SDK or any tracking-related functionality until you have provided your consent through the consent dialog. If you decline consent, no ads are requested and the advertising SDK is not initialized. If you select non-personalized ads, advertising identifiers may still be used by Google for limited purposes such as frequency capping, aggregated reporting, and fraud prevention, but not for ad targeting.

3.3 Information Stored Locally on Your Device

The App stores the following data locally on your device:

  • Game progress (current level, stars earned, best scores per level)
  • In-game currency balance (credits)
  • Inventory data (boosters, upgrades, purchased themes)
  • Gameplay statistics (total moves, total time played, win streaks)
  • App settings (volume levels, visual effects preferences, haptic feedback preference)

On Android, this local data may be automatically backed up to your personal Google Drive via Android Auto Backup — a Google operating-system feature that stores app data in a private folder on your Google Account (up to 25 MB per app). This backup is controlled by your device settings, not by us; we do not have access to its contents. You can disable Auto Backup at any time in Android Settings > Google > Backup. Independently, your data may also be synced via Google Play Games Services if you choose to sign in (see Section 3.4).

3.4 Information Synced via Google Play Games Services (Optional)

If you choose to sign into Google Play Games Services, the following data is saved to Google's cloud servers for the purpose of cloud saves and achievements:

  • Game progress and level completion data
  • Credits balance, inventory, and upgrade progress (including purchased themes and active theme)
  • Achievement unlock status
  • Gameplay statistics such as total moves and total time played
  • Win streak and login streak data
  • Story progression and decrypted-fragment data

Cloud sync via Google Play Games Services is optional. If you do not sign in, your data is not synced through Play Games — but, as noted in Section 3.3, it may still be backed up to your personal Google Drive via Android Auto Backup if that OS-level feature is enabled on your device.

3.5 Information We Do NOT Collect

The App does not collect:

  • Your name, email address, or phone number
  • Precise geolocation (GPS)
  • Contacts, photos, or files from your device
  • Camera, microphone, or biometric data
  • Financial information (payment processing is handled entirely by Google Play)
  • Health, fitness, or sensitive personal data

6. Advertising

The App displays advertisements through Google AdMob to support the free-to-play model. The App uses the following ad formats:

  • Rewarded ads — shown at your request in exchange for in-game rewards
  • Interstitial ads — shown between levels at natural break points
  • App open ads — shown when returning to the App after a period of inactivity

6.1 What the Advertising SDK Collects

Our advertising SDK (Google Mobile Ads SDK) may automatically collect and share the following data:

  • IP address — which may be used to estimate general (city-level) location
  • User interaction data — such as app launches, taps, and ad views
  • Diagnostic and performance data — app launch time, hang rate, energy usage (per Google's official SDK disclosure)
  • Device and account identifiers — including the Android Advertising ID (AAID) and app set ID

This data is used for purposes including advertising delivery, measurement, analytics, and fraud prevention. For full details, see the Google Mobile Ads SDK data disclosure.

6.2 Personalized vs. Non-Personalized Ads

If you are in the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you will be shown a consent dialog (compliant with IAB Transparency & Consent Framework v2.3) on first launch. You may choose to receive personalized or non-personalized ads, or decline advertising tracking entirely. You can change this preference at any time through the Settings screen in the App.

6.3 Opting Out of Ad Personalization

You can opt out of personalized advertising through any of these methods:

  • In-App: Settings > Privacy Settings (opens the consent management dialog)
  • Device Settings: Android Settings > Google > Ads > "Delete advertising ID" or "Opt out of Ads Personalization"
  • Purchasing the Root Access in-app purchase permanently removes interstitial and app-open ads (rewarded ads remain available, since they are shown only at your explicit request in exchange for in-game rewards)

16. Data Deletion

A full, step-by-step deletion guide — including the in-app one-tap PURGE flow, manual Play Games deletion through your Google Account, advertising-identifier reset, and an email-request pathway — is published at our dedicated Data & Account Deletion page. The summary below mirrors the main options:

  • Local data: Tap Settings → // DATA WIPE → PURGE inside the App, or uninstall the App / clear its data through Android Settings > Apps > Neural Breach > Storage > Clear Data.
  • Android Auto Backup: Manage or delete backups through Android Settings > Google > Backup (or your Google Drive account).
  • Cloud save data: The in-app PURGE attempts to delete the Play Games cloud-save snapshot on a best-effort basis. To remove it independently — for example, if you have already uninstalled the App or were signed out at the time of PURGE — sign in to Google Play Games and delete the data through your Google Account (see the Data & Account Deletion page, Method B).
  • Advertising ID: Reset or delete your advertising ID through Android Settings > Google > Ads.
  • Email deletion request: Contact us at support-komandakycto@proton.me and we will respond within 30 days (extendable by up to 60 days for complex requests). Because we operate no backend server, by email we can guide you to the correct self-service method and delete any data we control directly, but we cannot ourselves delete data that Google holds on your behalf (such as Play Games cloud saves, achievements, AdID, or Play Store purchase records).

Please note that certain data may be retained as required by applicable law (for example, purchase records for tax and accounting compliance) or for legitimate purposes (such as fraud prevention). We will inform you if we are unable to fully comply with a deletion request and explain the reasons.
